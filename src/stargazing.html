From the Villa

How to Stargaze at Gourdon

The villa sits above the Lot valley, well away from Toulouse's city glow. On clear July nights you can see the Milky Way arc overhead and the Summer Triangle high in the east. Give your eyes 15 minutes away from pool lights and phone screens; look south for Scorpius and the galactic core, west for Venus after sunset, and overhead for Vega, Deneb, and Altair.

July is the clearest month here. Check Météo-France the same afternoon. If it's clear after 22:00, head outside.

Wed · 8 July

Villa & Pool Day

Waning crescent Good

Moon low in late evening. Skies improving as the crescent sets.

What to see tonight

  • WVenus Brilliant after sunset, low on the horizon.
  • ESummer Triangle Vega, Deneb, and Altair rising through the evening.
  • SScorpius Antares visible once fully dark (~22:30).
  • SMilky Way Faint band, clearer toward the south-east.
Sat · 11 July

After Sarlat Market

Waning crescent Stormy

Thunderstorms earlier in the day. Skies may clear later — check conditions before heading out.

What to see tonight

  • WVenus Still the brightest object after sunset.
  • ↑Summer Triangle Overhead by late evening.
  • SScorpius & Antares Distinctive red giant low in the south.
  • SMilky Way core Faint glow toward Sagittarius.
  • EMars & Pleiades Pre-dawn bonus if you're up early.

Tonight's tip

Sagittarius and the galactic core hug the southern horizon until after 23:00. Wait for them to rise before tracing the Milky Way band.

Mon · 13 July

Post-Canoeing

Thin crescent Very good

A hair-thin moon. Excellent darkness for deep-sky viewing.

What to see tonight

  • SMilky Way Bright arc from south-east to north-west.
  • SScorpius Hook-shaped constellation with red Antares.
  • ↑Summer Triangle Vega (brightest), Deneb, and Altair.
  • WVenus Sets not long after twilight ends.
  • STeapot of Sagittarius Sits in the Milky Way core region.

Tonight's tip

With almost no moonlight, binoculars reveal far more structure in the Milky Way through Scorpius than the naked eye alone.

Tue · 14 July

Bastille Day, New Moon

New Moon Best night

The darkest night of the trip. No moonlight at all. If it's clear, this is the one not to miss.

What to see tonight

  • SMilky Way core Spectacular band through Scorpius and Sagittarius.
  • ↑Summer Triangle Dominates the eastern sky.
  • SScorpius Full scorpion shape visible from the villa.
  • WVenus Evening star before it sets.
  • SSaturn Rises late, visible in the south-east after midnight.

Tonight's tip

No moon means maximum darkness. Give your eyes 15 minutes away from white light before looking up. The galactic core looks richest around midnight when Sagittarius is highest in the south.

Wed · 15 July

After Cahors

1-day crescent Best night

A whisper of moon sets early, essentially as dark as last night for evening viewing.

What to see tonight

  • SMilky Way Same spectacular arc as Bastille Day.
  • ↑Summer Triangle Overhead all evening.
  • SScorpius & Sagittarius Heart of the galaxy toward the south.
  • WVenus Still bright in the west at dusk.
  • NBig Dipper Low in the north. Use it to find north.
Thu · 16 July

Final Full Villa Day

2-day crescent Best night

Young crescent low in the west. Still excellent for Milky Way viewing after it sets.

What to see tonight

  • SMilky Way Last best chance before departure.
  • ↑Summer Triangle Vega, Deneb, and Altair at their summer best.
  • SScorpius Antares and the curved tail.
  • WVenus & thin crescent Moon A beautiful pair at dusk.
  • SHercules Cluster (M13) Faint smudge with binoculars, high in the south-east.
